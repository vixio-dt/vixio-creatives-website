# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Public (players):** People in Shenzhen/GBA looking for cooperative game experiences. Groups of 4-6 friends or colleagues who want something more engaging than an escape room or walking tour. They arrive from social media, word-of-mouth, or search. They want to know what the experience is, see it in action, and book.

**B2B (venue operators):** Mall marketing directors and tenant engagement leads at commercial property groups in HK/GBA. They arrive from LinkedIn, industry referrals, or after seeing Vixio's public experience content. They want to understand what venue gamification means, see proof it works, and start a conversation about their space. Procurement is relationship-driven, not tender-driven.

## Product Purpose

Vixio Creatives is a venue gamification studio. The website serves two conversion paths: players book cooperative game sessions, and venue operators initiate B2B conversations. The site must demonstrate what Vixio does through its own design — a game-designed studio's website should feel interactive and playable, not static and corporate.

Success: players book sessions, venue operators fill the contact form, and the design process journal builds an audience of both players and industry professionals.

## Brand Personality

Direct, craft-driven, game-literate. A maker who directs, not a consultant who advises. Confident without being arrogant. Honest about stage without deficit framing. The voice is compressed, specific, problem-first. Think: a game designer explaining their work to a peer, not a salesperson pitching to a prospect.

Three words: **Craft. Tension. Play.**

## Anti-references

- **Generic agency sites** — grid of client logos, "we're passionate about creating meaningful experiences," stock photography, white-space-and-nothing design
- **Escape room websites** — dark backgrounds, neon green/purple, countdown timers, padlock imagery, horror aesthetics, cheap CG renders
- **Corporate consulting sites** — stock photos of diverse teams in glass conference rooms, blue gradients, "innovative solutions for modern challenges," icon grids

## Design Principles

1. **The site is playable.** The website itself should demonstrate what Vixio does. Interactive elements, physics-based responses, spatial relationships. The visitor is a participant, not a reader.

2. **Show the craft, not the pitch.** Lead with the work (footage, process, mechanics) rather than marketing claims. Let the design quality prove the studio's capability.

3. **Tension over comfort.** Good cooperative games create productive tension. The site should have moments of friction, surprise, and discovery — not smooth corporate predictability.

4. **Specifics do the work.** "4-6 players, 60-90 minutes, cooperative mechanics" over "an immersive interactive experience for groups." Numbers, mechanisms, and spatial details replace adjectives.

5. **Dual paths, one voice.** Players and venue operators see different content but experience the same design quality and brand voice. Neither path feels like a secondary citizen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as baseline
- Interactive/playable elements must have non-interactive fallbacks for screen readers and keyboard navigation
- Reduced motion preference respected (prefers-reduced-motion)
- Bilingual consideration: site launches in English, Chinese (traditional + simplified) support planned
- Color contrast ratios maintained even with warm cream backgrounds
