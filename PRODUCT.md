# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Buyers:** Adults 22-40 who value physical objects and shared experiences over screen time. Gift buyers looking for something premium and unique. Couples and friend groups who want to DO something together. Collectors who follow creators. They arrive from social media, creator channels, or search. They want to see what Vixio makes, feel the craft quality through the site itself, and buy or follow for launch.

**Creators:** Artists, illustrators, game designers, and IP holders looking for a production partner who can engineer premium physical products. They arrive from referrals, LinkedIn, or seeing Vixio's shipped products. They want to understand what collaboration looks like, see proof of production quality, and start a conversation.

## Product Purpose

Vixio is a creative studio that engineers premium physical products and playable experiences. The website serves two purposes: buyers discover and purchase products, and creators explore collaboration. The site must demonstrate what Vixio does through its own design: a studio that makes playable, tactile, premium things should have a website that feels playable, tactile, and premium.

Success: buyers purchase products or join the waitlist, creators reach out for collaboration, and the site itself proves Vixio's engineering and creative capability.

## Brand Personality

Direct, craft-driven, playful. A maker who engineers, not a consultant who advises. Confident without being arrogant. Honest about stage without deficit framing. The voice is compressed, specific, object-first. Think: a product designer showing you the thing they built, not a salesperson pitching you a vision.

Three words: **Craft. Play. Object.**

## Anti-references

- **Generic agency sites** — grid of client logos, "we're passionate about creating meaningful experiences," stock photography, white-space-and-nothing design
- **Escape room websites** — dark backgrounds, neon green/purple, countdown timers, padlock imagery, horror aesthetics
- **Corporate consulting sites** — stock photos, blue gradients, "innovative solutions for modern challenges," icon grids
- **Dropshipping stores** — cheap product grids, "limited time offer," urgency manipulation
- **AI-generated product mockups** — floating objects on gradient backgrounds, obviously fake renders

## Design Principles

1. **The site is playable.** The website itself should demonstrate what Vixio does. Interactive elements, physics-based responses, spatial relationships. The visitor is a participant, not a reader.

2. **Show the craft, not the pitch.** Lead with the work (objects, process, engineering) rather than marketing claims. Let the design quality prove the studio's capability.

3. **Tactile over slick.** The site should feel physical: weight, friction, texture. Elements behave like objects on a table, not pixels on a screen. Warm, not clinical.

4. **Specifics do the work.** "Premium plush, mystery kits, playable experiences" over "an innovative creative studio." Materials, mechanisms, and details replace adjectives.

5. **Playfulness with purpose.** Micro-interactions reward curiosity. Discovery moments surprise without confusing. The playfulness IS the proof of capability.

## Positioning

**Main:** Vixio is a creative studio that engineers premium physical products and playable experiences — crafted to be held, opened, solved, and shared.

**Supporting principle:** Physical-first, digitally enhanced. The object leads; digital serves.

**Short version:** Premium physical products and playable experiences.

## Product Lines

- Premium physical products with creator collaborators (plush, collectibles, objects)
- Playable experience products (mystery kits, puzzle boxes, DIY builds with digital layer)
- Venue experiences (future, Phase 3)

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance as baseline
- Interactive/playable elements must have non-interactive fallbacks for screen readers and keyboard navigation
- Reduced motion preference respected (prefers-reduced-motion)
- Bilingual consideration: site launches in English, Chinese (traditional + simplified) support planned
- Color contrast ratios maintained even with warm cream backgrounds
