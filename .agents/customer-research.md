## 3. What Creators Need (Pain Points and Opportunities)

### 3A. The Production Gap

This is Vixio's core value proposition. The research confirms the gap is real and specific:

**What indie creators can do:**
- Design characters and worlds (digital)
- Self-publish art books and prints
- Sell at conventions (doujin stalls, art toy expos)
- Use print-on-demand for basic merch (t-shirts, mugs, stickers)

**What indie creators cannot easily do:**
- Manufacture designer toys (vinyl, resin, PVC) at quality
- Access supply chains (e.g., Dongguan factories require minimum orders and relationships)
- Manage production pipelines (prototyping, tooling, QC, packaging)
- Distribute physical products beyond convention sales and online stores
- Scale without becoming a production company instead of a creator

**The POD trap:** Print-on-demand solves the inventory problem but creates a quality/margin problem. Standard POD margins are 15-20%. Products are generic (same blanks as everyone else). For creators who care about craft, POD flattens their IP onto commodity products — exactly the "logo on a mug" problem Vixio identifies.

**The Pop Mart path:** Creators who do get into physical production typically go through Pop Mart or similar partners, but the terms are restrictive:
- 8-15% royalty (tiered by volume)
- 24-month exclusive contracts
- Pop Mart holds exclusive production/distribution rights during contract
- Non-compete with Funko, MediCom, etc. during term
- 10-14 month timeline from submission to launch
- IP ownership retained, but specific toy designs are locked

**What Vixio offers differently:**
- Full production partnership without exclusive lock-in (assumption from PMC — confirm terms)
- Creator name stays on the product
- No blind-box/gacha mechanics
- Supply chain access (Dongguan network) that individual creators cannot access
- Quality bar that matches the creator's own standard

### 3B. How Indie Creators Currently Monetize IP

1. **Convention sales:** Direct-to-consumer at ACGHK, art toy expos, illustration fairs. Low overhead but limited reach and one-time revenue.
2. **Online stores:** Instagram shops, personal websites, platforms like zbfghk.org (for comics/art books). Limited by marketing reach.
3. **Print-on-demand:** Redbubble, Printify, Fourthwall. Low risk, low margin (15-20%), generic product quality.
4. **Licensing deals:** Pop Mart, brand collaborations. Higher revenue but loss of creative control and long exclusivity.
5. **Self-publishing:** Art books, zines, graphic novels. Strong in HK illustration community (Little Thunder model).
6. **Commissions:** Client work and freelance illustration. Trades time for money, not IP-building.
7. **Government grants:** GEPS (HK$500K), Cyberport (HK$500K+200K), DLAB (booth + training). Critical for survival but not a business model.

**Income reality:** More than 50% of creators globally earn less than USD $15,000/year. HK's high cost of living makes this particularly acute.

### 3C. What the A24 Model Looks Like for Creators

A24's approach (which Vixio explicitly references as an analog):
- Filmmakers accept lower upfront fees in exchange for creative freedom and artistic autonomy
- The studio's reputation becomes a draw — creators seek out A24, not the other way around
- Shared risk model: equity partners, co-producers help fund ambitious work
- Performance milestones align returns with success
- YouTube creators building "coherent fictional universes" are now viable A24 candidates

**Translation to Vixio's pitch:** The creator trades some upfront revenue for creative integrity, production capability, and label association. The label's taste becomes the draw. Early partners get the best terms because they help build the label's reputation.

### 3D. Market Tailwinds

- **Art toy market:** Projected $50B globally. Mainland China alone: 169B yuan growing to 300B yuan by 2029, 520M consumers.
- **HK government support:** CCIDA funded 8 art toy projects totaling ~HK$38M since 2022. Year-long "Tribute to Art Toys" celebration in 2026. Finance Chief Paul Chan announced support for 30+ cultural IP projects over 5 years.
- **IP design registrations rising:** 31 applications (2022) to 59 applications (2024) in games/toys category.
- **ACGHK expanding:** Exhibition space nearly doubled from 19,890 sqm to 34,872 sqm in 2025. 610+ toy designers.
- **GBA integration:** No content quotas for HK animation/TV imported to mainland. Cross-border talent exchange active.
- **EY data (from PMC):** 48% of HK consumers purchased local entertainment, 84% plan to. Gen Z least cost-sensitive, rates unique (25% vs 17%) and premium (21% vs 17%) higher.
