# Content Strategy: Vixio Creatives

**Purpose:** Practical content playbook for building visibility before and during creator outreach. Not a branding document.

**Timeline:** 4-week pre-launch cadence starting now. Creator outreach is live.

**Last updated:** 2026-05-18

---

## 1. Platform Strategy

### Instagram

**Why:** Primary portfolio platform for HK visual creators. Art toy designers, illustrators, and character designers live here. Little Thunder has 1M+ followers. This is where creators will check Vixio before responding to a cold email. If the grid looks empty or generic, the outreach dies.

**What to post:**
- Production process shots (prototypes, materials, factory visits)
- Creator spotlights (artists Vixio admires, with commentary on why)
- Design philosophy (single-image + caption posts explaining decisions)
- Reels: short-form behind-the-scenes (15-30s, no narration needed, process footage with text overlay)
- Carousel posts: visual essays (e.g., "5 things we look for in a collaboration")

**Posting frequency:** 3-4 posts per week. 2-3 Stories per day during active production phases.

**Tone:** Social media register from the brand voice profile. Compressed. Visual-first. One idea per post. No hashtag pileups. The caption supports the image, not the other way around.

**Content types split:** 40% production/craft, 30% creator world/IP, 20% philosophy/standard, 10% scene/community.

**Grid strategy:** First 9 posts must tell a coherent story. A creator landing on the profile should understand within 5 seconds: this is a creative label, they make physical products tied to IPs, and they care about craft. No logo tiles, no quote graphics, no stock imagery.

---

### Xiaohongshu (RED)

**Why:** Massive in HK and GBA. Art toy collectors are extremely active here. This is the discovery platform for physical collectibles in the Chinese-speaking market. Critical for reaching creators who target mainland audiences and for GBA expansion. If Vixio wants to be taken seriously in the art toy space, RED presence is non-negotiable.

**What to post:**
- Product close-ups with detailed craft notes (material, weight, finish, production choices)
- Factory/production process content (this performs exceptionally well on RED)
- Art toy market commentary (trends, events, what's worth collecting and why)
- Repost-worthy visual content: unboxing-style reveals, comparison shots (prototype vs final)
- Bilingual captions (Traditional Chinese primary, English secondary) or Chinese-only

**Posting frequency:** 2-3 posts per week. Consistency matters more than volume on RED.

**Tone:** More detailed and educational than Instagram. RED users expect substance. Show the process, explain the choices. The craft voice from the brand profile works well here, but slightly warmer and more explanatory.

**Content types split:** 50% production/craft (this is what performs on RED), 30% product/IP reveals, 20% scene/community.

**Note:** RED requires a Chinese-language content capability. If Denis cannot write fluent Chinese copy, this platform needs a collaborator or should be deprioritized to Phase 2 (post-launch). Bad Chinese copy is worse than no Chinese copy.

---

### LinkedIn

**Why:** B2B outreach channel. Where studio founders, industry professionals, and potential hires will vet Denis and Vixio. Not a content platform for Vixio; a professional credibility platform for Denis personally. The LinkedIn profile IS the outreach infrastructure.

**What to post (Denis's personal profile):**
- Industry observations (the production gap for indie creators, the art toy market in HK)
- Behind-the-scenes of building a creative label (honest, first-person, founder's journey)
- Commentary on relevant industry news (Pop Mart earnings, ACGHK announcements, CCIDA funding)
- Signal posts: what Vixio is looking for in collaborators (without being a job ad)

**Posting frequency:** 1-2 posts per week. Quality over quantity. LinkedIn rewards thoughtful long-form.

**Tone:** Creator register from the brand voice profile but in first person. Denis speaking directly. Professional but not corporate. Opinionated. "Here's what I think about X and here's why." Not "We're excited to announce."

**Content types split:** 40% industry commentary, 30% founder journey, 20% creator ecosystem observations, 10% hiring/collaboration signals.

---

### Twitter/X

**Why:** Global creative community, tech/startup adjacent audience, indie game dev community. Lower priority than Instagram and LinkedIn for HK-specific outreach, but useful for international visibility and connecting with game studios, animators, and globally-facing creators. Also where media and press discover stories.

**What to post:**
- Sharp, compressed takes on the creator economy and IP production (the "tension" voice trait works well here)
- Quote-tweet and engage with creator content (build relationships publicly)
- Thread format: longer arguments about the label model, honest commerce, anti-blind-box position
- Link back to Instagram for visual content

**Posting frequency:** 3-5 tweets per week. Low effort per tweet. Conversational engagement matters more than original posts.

**Tone:** Most compressed version of the Vixio voice. Fragments work. Strong opinions encouraged. This is where "A merch company prints your logo on their products. A label invests in your world." lives naturally.

**Content types split:** 40% commentary/takes, 30% engagement with creators, 20% link-backs to visual content, 10% industry news.

---

### Platform Priority Order

1. **Instagram** — must-have for creator credibility. Build first.
2. **LinkedIn** — must-have for outreach. Denis's profile optimization is Day 1.
3. **Twitter/X** — nice-to-have for broader visibility. Low effort to maintain.
4. **Xiaohongshu** — high-value but requires Chinese-language capability. Phase 2 unless Denis can produce Chinese content now.

## 2. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: The Craft

**Description:** Behind-the-scenes of production, manufacturing, and design process. How things are made and why specific choices were made. Materials, prototypes, dead ends, quality decisions. This is the pillar that proves Vixio is real, not vaporware.

**Why it matters:** Creators evaluating a partnership need to see production capability. Collectors want to understand what makes an artifact worth the price. This is the "show your work" pillar.

**Example posts:**

1. Photo of material samples (resin types, vinyl grades) with caption: "Three resin options for the first collaboration. Chose the heaviest. If it doesn't feel right in the hand, we don't ship it."
2. Before/after: prototype vs revised prototype. "First sample came back wrong. The weight felt cheap. Switched from hollow-cast to solid resin at 2x the unit cost."
3. Carousel: "What we look for in a factory partner" (5 slides, each with one criterion and a photo).
4. Video (15s): Denis at a Dongguan factory, no narration, just ambient sound and process footage. Text overlay: "Where it gets built."
5. Photo of rejected samples in a row. Caption: "These didn't ship. The goosebump standard is a filter, not a slogan."

**Platform fit:** Instagram (primary), RED (strong), LinkedIn (occasional, more reflective framing).

**Frequency:** 2 posts per week across platforms.

---

### Pillar 2: The World

**Description:** Spotlighting creator IPs, character stories, and world-building. Celebrating the depth of the worlds Vixio works with. This pillar is outward-facing, not about Vixio's process but about the creators and their universes.

**Why it matters:** Positions Vixio as a curator with taste. Shows creators that Vixio understands and respects IP at the world level, not just the product level. Shows fans that every release has a real story behind it.

**Example posts:**

1. Spotlight on a creator Vixio admires (even without a partnership): "The work of [creator name]. Characters with arcs. Environments with logic. The kind of world that earns physical form."
2. Character study: a single character from a collaboration, photographed as an artifact with a short narrative from the creator about the character's origin.
3. "Worlds we're watching" series: short posts highlighting indie IPs, art toy designers, or illustrators whose work demonstrates what Vixio looks for.
4. Creator Q&A (text-based, carousel format): 5 questions, 5 answers, with the creator's own artwork as backgrounds.
5. Side-by-side: the original digital art and the physical artifact. "From screen to shelf. Same world, new medium."

**Platform fit:** Instagram (primary), Twitter (commentary format), LinkedIn (industry framing).

**Frequency:** 1-2 posts per week. Cannot run this pillar heavily until collaborations are further along. Pre-launch, lean on "worlds we're watching" (curation without claiming credit).

---

### Pillar 3: The Standard

**Description:** What the "goosebump standard" means in practice. Quality decisions, rejected paths, the difference between good enough and right. This is the philosophical backbone of the content strategy.

**Why it matters:** Differentiates Vixio from every other label or merch company. The standard is the brand. Without articulating it, Vixio is just another production house with nice words.

**Example posts:**

1. "A merch company prints your logo on their products. A label invests in your world." (text post, bold typography on a clean background)
2. Photo of a detail (paint application, joint mechanism, packaging insert) with a caption explaining why this specific detail matters.
3. "What we won't do" post: "No blind boxes. No gacha. No manufactured grind. You know what you're getting. That's the deal."
4. Long-form LinkedIn post: "Why we price transparently" — the case against artificial scarcity and random-pull mechanics.
5. Instagram caption essay: "The through-line is taste, not genre. The medium is not locked. What stays constant is the standard."

**Platform fit:** LinkedIn (long-form philosophy), Instagram (visual proof), Twitter (compressed takes).

**Frequency:** 1 post per week. This pillar needs to be concentrated to avoid preachiness. Show, don't tell, most of the time. Reserve explicit philosophy posts for moments they'll land.

---

### Pillar 4: The Scene

**Description:** HK and GBA art toy and creator ecosystem. Events, community, industry observations. Vixio as a participant in the scene, not just a brand broadcasting at it.

**Why it matters:** Positions Denis and Vixio within the existing creative community rather than above or outside it. Builds goodwill. Creates natural connection points with creators before direct outreach.

**Example posts:**

1. Event coverage: photos and observations from ACGHK, art toy expos, PMQ visits. "Walked the doujin zone at ACGHK. 550+ independent stalls. This is where the worlds are."
2. "HK is the birthplace of the designer toy movement. It's not history, it's still happening." (with photos of local art toy culture)
3. Repost/spotlight: sharing other creators' work with genuine commentary (not performative support, actual insight about what makes the work good).
4. Industry data post: "Art toy market projected at $50B globally. 520M consumers in mainland China alone. The tailwind is real." (LinkedIn format)
5. Behind-the-scenes of a space: "JCCAC. Nine stories of artist studios in a converted factory. The kind of place where worlds get built." (Instagram carousel)

**Platform fit:** Instagram (visual event content), LinkedIn (industry commentary), Twitter (scene observations), RED (event coverage for Chinese audience).

**Frequency:** 1-2 posts per week. Ramps up around events.

---

### Pillar 5: Honest Commerce

**Description:** Why no blind boxes. What transparent pricing means. The label philosophy applied to how things are sold, not just how they're made. Anti-gacha, anti-manufactured-scarcity, pro-"you know what you're buying."

**Why it matters:** This is the most contrarian and shareable pillar. In a market dominated by blind-box mechanics (Pop Mart), taking an explicit stand against it is both a differentiator and a magnet for creators and fans who feel the same way.

**Example posts:**

1. "Pop Mart artists get 8-15% royalty on 24-month exclusive contracts. We think creators deserve a better deal." (LinkedIn, data-backed)
2. "Blind boxes turn collectors into gamblers. We make objects you choose because you want them, not because you're chasing a 1-in-144 variant." (Twitter thread)
3. Pricing transparency post: "What goes into the price of an artifact: materials, labor, creator royalty, label margin. No mystery box markup." (Instagram carousel with breakdown)
4. "Limited drops. Honest pricing. No tricks." (Simple text graphic, Instagram)
5. "You know what you're getting. That's the deal." (Story-format, repeated as a recurring sign-off)

**Platform fit:** Twitter (debates and takes), LinkedIn (long-form arguments), Instagram (visual statements).

**Frequency:** 1 post per week. Strong opinions need spacing or they become a lecture.

## 3. Pre-Launch Content Calendar (4 Weeks)

### Context

Denis is doing creator outreach NOW. Content must support that. The goal is not virality or follower counts. The goal is: when a creator gets a cold email or DM from Denis, they check the Instagram profile and LinkedIn, and what they find makes them take the conversation seriously.

Success criteria for the 4-week plan: a creator who visits the Instagram grid sees at least 12 posts that communicate taste, production capability, and a clear philosophy. A creator who visits Denis's LinkedIn sees a professional who understands their world.

---

### Week 1: Establish Presence (Days 1-7)

**Goal:** Accounts exist, look intentional, and communicate the basics. Denis's profiles are optimized. The grid has enough content to not look empty.

**Instagram (5 posts):**

1. **Grid post 1:** Hero image. The strongest visual Vixio has. A production prototype, a material sample, or a studio shot. Caption: Label name, one-liner, and "First collaboration in production." No hashtag essay.
2. **Grid post 2:** Close-up of a material or process. Caption explaining the choice. Craft pillar.
3. **Grid post 3:** Text-on-image. "Characters and worlds worth believing in." Clean typography, brand colors. Standard pillar.
4. **Grid post 4:** Photo of the workspace, studio, or production environment. Caption: what Vixio is and what it does, in two sentences. Scene pillar.
5. **Grid post 5:** A creator's work that Denis genuinely admires (with proper credit). Caption: what makes this work good, from a craft perspective. World pillar. (Gets engagement from the creator's audience.)

**Stories (daily):** Behind-the-scenes fragments. Low production value is fine. Studio, materials, references, mood boards.

**LinkedIn (Denis personal, 2 posts):**

1. Profile announcement: "I'm building Vixio Creatives, a creative label for entertainment IPs in Hong Kong." First-person, founder's journey framing. What it is, why it exists, what the first collaboration is. Not a press release.
2. Industry observation: something about the HK creator ecosystem, the production gap, or the art toy market. Data-backed. Opinionated.

**Twitter (3 tweets):**

1. Bio update + pinned tweet introducing Vixio.
2. Sharp take on the creator economy or IP production.
3. Engage with 5+ creators' tweets. No selling, just genuine response to their work.

**RED:** Skip this week unless Chinese content is ready.

---

### Week 2: Show the Philosophy (Days 8-14)

**Goal:** Anyone checking the profiles now understands not just what Vixio is, but what it believes. The honest commerce and quality standard are visible.

**Instagram (3-4 posts):**

1. **"What we won't do" post.** Carousel or text graphic. "No blind boxes. No gacha. No manufactured grind. You know what you're getting." Honest Commerce pillar.
2. **Production detail shot.** A specific quality decision with context. "Switched from X to Y because Z." Craft pillar.
3. **"Worlds we're watching" post 1.** Spotlight a creator/IP that embodies what Vixio looks for. World pillar.
4. **Reel (optional):** 15-second process video. No narration, text overlay, ambient sound. Craft pillar.

**LinkedIn (1-2 posts):**

1. "Why no blind boxes." Long-form post. The case against random-pull mechanics. Data on Pop Mart terms (8-15% royalty, 24-month exclusives). What honest commerce means. Honest Commerce pillar.
2. Optional: "What I look for in a collaboration" — Denis's personal criteria.

**Twitter (3-5 tweets):**

1. Thread: "The production gap for indie creators." Summarize the problem Vixio solves. 5-7 tweets. Scene + Standard pillar.
2. Engage with art toy community content.
3. Share an industry stat with commentary.

---

### Week 3: Show the Capability (Days 15-21)

**Goal:** Proof that Vixio can build. Production shots, factory relationships, material knowledge. This week is the "we are real" week. Critical for outreach credibility.

**Instagram (3-4 posts):**

1. **Factory/production shot.** The Dongguan network in action. Materials, machinery, prototypes. Caption: what this relationship means for creators. Craft pillar.
2. **Before/after or iteration shot.** A prototype that evolved. Show the revision process. "This is what quality control looks like when the standard is goosebumps." Craft + Standard pillar.
3. **Creator spotlight post 2.** Another "Worlds we're watching." Different creator, different medium. Demonstrates range of taste. World pillar.
4. **Carousel: "How a collaboration works."** 4-5 slides walking through Partner → Create → Release. Keep it visual, not text-heavy. Craft pillar.

**LinkedIn (1-2 posts):**

1. "Building a supply chain as an indie label." First-person account of accessing Dongguan manufacturing as a small operation. Practical, honest, specific. Craft + Scene pillar.
2. Optional: share a data point about the art toy market with commentary.

**Twitter (3-5 tweets):**

1. "A24 proved taste-as-brand for film. Pop Mart proved it for designer toys. We asked: what does this model look like for creator-led physical IP?" (Thread or standalone.)
2. Share a production photo with minimal caption.
3. Engage with game dev or animation communities.

---

### Week 4: Invite Collaboration (Days 22-28)

**Goal:** Transition from broadcasting to inviting. The profiles now have enough credibility for an explicit call: we're looking for collaborators. Outreach emails reference this content.

**Instagram (3-4 posts):**

1. **Direct invitation post.** "Looking for creators with worlds worth bringing into physical form. You bring the vision. We handle the build. Your name stays on it." Clear, no ambiguity. Link to website or contact. World + Standard pillar.
2. **"What we offer" carousel.** Creative direction, design, manufacturing, distribution. One per slide, each with a supporting image. Not a sales deck. Craft pillar.
3. **Creator spotlight post 3.** Continuing the "Worlds we're watching" series. This one can be a creator you're actively reaching out to (with their permission). World pillar.
4. **Reel: 30-second studio/process montage.** Everything from Weeks 1-3 cut together. End card: "First collaboration in production." Craft pillar.

**LinkedIn (2 posts):**

1. **Collaboration announcement post.** "Vixio Creatives is open for collaboration. Here's what we're looking for and what we offer." Detailed, professional, first-person. Link to website.
2. **"What makes a good label partner" post.** Denis's perspective on what he looks for in a creator partnership. Not a job ad. A statement of values.

**Twitter (3-5 tweets):**

1. "If you're a creator with an IP that deserves physical form, DM me." Direct.
2. Quote-tweet creator content with "This is the kind of world-building we want to partner with."
3. Engage in conversations about creator economics, IP licensing, physical products.

### Social Proof Without Shipped Products

This is the hardest part. No product, no testimonials, no sales numbers. Here's what can substitute:

**Structural proof:**
- The A24/Pop Mart model comparison. "The model is proven. The application to creator-led physical IP is new." This is an argument, not a testimonial.
- Market data. "Art toy market: $50B globally. 520M consumers in mainland China. 48% of HK consumers purchased local entertainment." Numbers are more credible than adjectives.
- Supply chain references. "Dongguan manufacturing network" signals real capability without requiring a portfolio.

**Process proof:**
- Production photos. Prototypes, materials, factory visits. This is the most powerful substitute for finished products. A photo of resin samples on a workshop table says more than any claim.
- Iteration documentation. "First sample came back wrong. Here's what we changed." Shows standards without needing a finished product.

**Network proof:**
- Creator spotlights. Curating and commenting on other creators' work demonstrates taste and industry knowledge.
- Event attendance documentation. Photos from ACGHK, art toy expos, PMQ. Shows Denis is embedded in the scene.
- Industry association references. HKBTA, HKSI, DLAB familiarity signals insider knowledge.

**Honest pre-launch framing:**
- "First collaboration in production. The label is only as real as what it ships." This framing is more credible than silence. Own the stage, don't hide from it.

## 5. Content Production Notes

### What Denis Can Produce Solo

**Text posts:** LinkedIn long-form, Instagram captions, tweets, all platform bios and profile copy. Denis writes in the brand voice naturally (it's his voice formalized). No collaborator needed.

**Photography (phone-quality):** Studio shots, material close-ups, workspace photos, event coverage. Modern phones produce Instagram-grade images. Natural light, clean backgrounds, close framing. No studio setup needed.

**Short-form video (15-30s):** Process footage, studio walkthroughs, material handling. Shoot on phone, vertical format. No narration needed. Text overlay in post. CapCut or Instagram's native editor for basic cuts.

**Carousels and text graphics:** Canva or Figma for simple typography-on-background posts. Keep templates minimal. 2-3 templates max, reuse them.

**Stories:** Daily behind-the-scenes. Lowest production bar. Phone camera, no editing, post directly.

### What Needs Collaborators

**Product photography (hero shots):** When finished products exist, professional photography with controlled lighting, styled backgrounds, and multiple angles. This is NOT a Day 1 need. Budget for it when the first collaboration is ready for announcement.

**Video (60s+):** Anything with editing, motion graphics, sound design, or narrative structure. A 60-second brand video or creator interview video needs at minimum a videographer and editor. Not a priority for pre-launch.

**Chinese-language content:** Xiaohongshu copy, bilingual captions, Chinese social media management. If Denis doesn't write fluent Chinese, this needs a collaborator.

**Graphic design:** If the brand needs consistent visual templates beyond what Canva/Figma can do, or if the Instagram grid needs a cohesive design system, a graphic designer for a one-time template creation session (not ongoing) would help.

### Tools and Workflow

| Tool | Use | Cost |
|------|-----|------|
| Phone camera | All daily photo/video content | Free |
| Canva (free tier) | Text graphics, carousels, simple templates | Free |
| CapCut | Short-form video editing, text overlays | Free |
| Later or Buffer (free tier) | Schedule posts across platforms | Free |
| Notion or simple spreadsheet | Content calendar tracking | Free |
| Instagram native tools | Stories, Reels editing | Free |
| LinkedIn native editor | Long-form posts | Free |

Total cost for tools: $0. The constraint is Denis's time, not budget.

### Time Investment Estimate Per Week

| Activity | Time | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| Content planning (weekly batch) | 1 hour | Sunday or Monday. Plan the week's posts. |
| Photography/video capture | 2 hours | Batch shoot. 30 min per shoot, 4 shoots per week. Capture during natural workflow (factory visits, studio time). |
| Writing captions and posts | 2 hours | Batch write. Instagram captions, LinkedIn posts, tweets. |
| Editing and scheduling | 1 hour | Crop, filter, text overlays, schedule via Later/Buffer. |
| Community engagement | 1.5 hours | 15-20 min per day. Comment, reply, engage with creators. This is NOT optional. |
| LinkedIn engagement | 1 hour | Comment on others' posts, respond to messages. |

**Total: ~8.5 hours per week.**

This is significant for a solo founder also doing outreach, production, and everything else. Realistic reduction strategies:

- **Weeks 1-2 are heavier** (establishing presence, building initial grid). Budget 10-12 hours.
- **Weeks 3-4 can taper to 6-7 hours** as templates and rhythms are established.
- **Batch everything.** One photo shoot produces 3-4 posts. One writing session produces a week of captions.
- **Skip RED until a Chinese content collaborator is onboard.** That saves 2-3 hours per week.
- **Reuse across platforms.** An Instagram carousel becomes a LinkedIn post becomes a tweet thread. Same content, different register.

## 6. Metrics That Matter (and When to Check Them)

Do not obsess over metrics during pre-launch. The goal is not growth. The goal is credibility when checked.

**Check after Week 4:**

- Profile visits from target creators (Instagram insights: "Accounts reached" filtered by non-followers)
- LinkedIn profile views (should spike during outreach weeks)
- DM/message response rate from outreach targets
- Cold email reply rate correlated with "checked our Instagram first" (ask in conversations)

**Do not track yet:**
- Follower count (irrelevant at this stage)
- Engagement rate (too small a sample)
- Reach/impressions (meaningless without a baseline)

**The real metric:** When a creator responds to a cold email with "I checked out your Instagram, let's talk," the content strategy is working.
