## Usage Notes

### Sending Platform
- Art toy designers and illustrators: Instagram DM first (most are active there), email follow-up
- Game studios: Email or LinkedIn (more professional context)
- Hiring: LinkedIn InMail or direct email

### Personalization Checklist
Before sending each email, fill in:
- [ ] Recipient's first name
- [ ] Their character/IP/game name
- [ ] One specific, genuine observation about their work (this is the most important line in the email — generic compliments get ignored)
- [ ] Why their specific work caught your eye (not generic "I love your art")
- [ ] Any relevant current project, upcoming show, or recent achievement for the final follow-up

### Timing
- Email 1: Send
- Email 2: 3 days after Email 1 (5 days for hiring sequence)
- Email 3: 5 days after Email 2

### What Not to Do
- Do not send the same personalization line to multiple creators ("your characters are so unique")
- Do not attach a pitch deck to the first email
- Do not mention competitors by name in the email (save that for the conversation)
- Do not use "excited to reach out" or any variation
- Do not frame Vixio's stage as a weakness ("we're just starting out but...")
- Do not promise specific terms in the email — that's for the conversation

### Response Handling
- If they reply with interest: propose a specific time for a call, share a brief one-pager about Vixio if asked
- If they reply with questions: answer directly, don't redirect to a website or document
- If they reply "not now": acknowledge, ask if you can follow up in [timeframe], add to future outreach list
- If no response after Sequence: wait 30 days, then a single "checking in" note if there's a new development to share (e.g., first collaboration launched, new relevant market data)
