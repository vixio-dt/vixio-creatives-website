# Competitor Battle Cards

Sales reference for Vixio Creatives. One card per competitor type. Use when a prospect mentions a name or describes a comparable experience.

---

## 1. vs SCRAP (Tokyo, Japan)

**What they do:** Invented the Real Escape Game in 2007. Produces 60-70 new puzzle-based escape games per year with 80+ anime/manga IP collaborations (Detective Conan, Evangelion, Attack on Titan). Operates 16 permanent venues across Japan with 830 employees.

**Their strengths:**
- Category inventor with 63.9% brand recognition among first-time players in Japan
- 450+ original game titles; 15M+ cumulative participants
- Deep IP relationships (inbound-only -- IP holders approach them)
- Revenue of ~$30M (Y4.52B), growing +37% over 2 years
- Diversified revenue: tickets, merchandise (Y650M/yr), corporate, publishing, weddings

**Their weaknesses:**
- Paper-based, low-tech format unchanged in 15+ years -- no projection, no AR/VR, no sensor-based interactivity
- CEO Kato described digital transformation as "telling a baseball pitcher with a hurt shoulder to play soccer" -- cultural resistance to technology
- Independent review: SCRAP games can be reproduced with "paper and a printer"
- 76.5% of workforce is part-time, creating quality consistency issues
- US expansion failed (San Francisco closed 2020); overwhelmingly Japan-dependent
- Employee ratings: work-life balance 2.2/5, compensation 2.7/5

**When prospects mention them:** "We saw what SCRAP does with Detective Conan at USJ -- we want something like that for our venue."

**Vixio's counter-positioning:** SCRAP proves the game format works -- 15M people have played their games. But their experiences look like they were designed in 2007 because they were. No cinematic production, no dynamic lighting, no immersive sound design. We build the same depth of cooperative game mechanics with directed cinematic production value. Think SCRAP's puzzle craft meets Moment Factory's visual register.

**Key differentiator:** SCRAP does games without cinema. We do both.

**Data points:** Y4.52B revenue, 830 employees (195 FT / 635 PT), 450+ titles, 80+ IP collabs, 3-10% escape rate, Detective Conan alone: 2.3M participants / ~Y6.9B cumulative ticket revenue.

## 2. vs Moment Factory (Montreal, Canada)

**What they do:** World-class cinematic AV studio producing projection mapping, LED installations, and immersive environments. 550+ projects across 6 continents since 2001. Built the gamification platform for Super Nintendo World. Lumina Night Walks across 25+ global locations with 7M+ tickets sold.

**Their strengths:**
- 450-480 full-time employees across Montreal, Paris, Tokyo, New York, Singapore
- Proprietary tech stack: X-Agora OS, NVIDIA Omniverse, Unreal Engine
- Revenue estimated $140-180M; profitable
- Named Fast Company's Most Innovative Companies 3 times
- Originals division (Lumina, AURA) creating scalable IP with recurring revenue
- AURA at Notre-Dame: 715,000+ visitors from 45 countries

**Their weaknesses:**
- No game design depth -- "Augmented Games" is motion-reactive mini-games without narrative, puzzle depth, or consequence
- Accurately described as "a technology platform in search of game design"
- Custom projects average $500K-$5M+, pricing out mid-market clients
- Pivoting away from small bespoke projects toward Originals, leaving $30K-$250K segment underserved
- 550 projects over 23 years = only ~24/year; bespoke model has a throughput ceiling
- Glassdoor: 3.7/5 overall, compensation complaints, burnout and turnover cited
- Audiences increasingly reject passive projection-based spectacle -- 5 major Las Vegas immersive venues closed 2024-2026

**When prospects mention them:** "We want a Moment Factory-level production in our space, but we can't afford their rates."

**Vixio's counter-positioning:** Moment Factory makes spaces look extraordinary. But audiences walk through, look up, and leave. There is no game, no agency, no reason to come back. Their Augmented Games platform proves they know they need interactivity -- but game design is not something you bolt on to a spectacle studio. We start from game mechanics and add cinematic production. The result is something people play, not just watch. And we operate at project sizes Moment Factory no longer pursues.

**Key differentiator:** Moment Factory does cinema without games. We do both.

**Data points:** $140-180M revenue, 450-480 FT employees, 550+ projects, 7M+ Lumina tickets, typical project $500K-$5M+, first external equity in 23 years taken Sept 2024.

## 3. vs LOST HK (Hong Kong)

**What they do:** Hong Kong's leading tech-forward escape room operator. 6 HK branches plus 12 international locations. Tech-mediated multi-room escape games with automatic triggers and electronic mechanisms. Licensed IP rooms include Batman, LEGO, and The Hunger Games. Also operates LOST Junior (STEAM, ages 6-14) and LuminCube arena format.

**Their strengths:**
- Market leader in HK escape rooms since 2013
- Strong B2B client list (HSBC, Google, Meta mentioned in broader market)
- TripAdvisor 4.4/5 (Causeway Bay), multilingual staff
- Franchise model ("Escape-as-a-Service") with international reach
- Multiple formats: standard escape, Junior, LuminCube, Role Play Escape (LARP hybrid)

**Their weaknesses:**
- Still fundamentally escape rooms -- puzzle-forward, not cinematic or narrative-driven
- Some older rooms described as worn/needing maintenance; mechanical failures noted
- 45 minutes feels short for the price (HK$170-250/pp)
- "No sense of ending" criticism in reviews -- weak narrative resolution
- No IP creation capability; licensed IP rooms are static adaptations, not dynamic experiences
- No projection mapping, no immersive sound design, no cinematic production value

**When prospects mention them:** "We already work with LOST for corporate team-building. Why would we try something different?"

**Vixio's counter-positioning:** LOST is a good escape room operator. But escape rooms are a solved format -- 45 minutes, padlock-to-padlock, one-and-done. We design cooperative experiences with cinematic production, serialized engagement (reasons to return), and measurable outcomes for venue operators: foot traffic routing, dwell time increase, repeat visit rate. An escape room fills a room. We activate a zone.

**Key differentiator:** LOST operates rooms. We design systems that drive measurable venue KPIs.

**Data points:** 6 HK branches, 12 international, HK$170-250/pp, 45-min sessions, TripAdvisor 4.4/5 (150 reviews), Batman/LEGO/Hunger Games IP rooms, founded 2013.

## 4. vs Luna Entertainment (UK)

**What they do:** UK-based IP-led experience operator backed by Sony Music Masterworks. Licenses blockbuster entertainment brands (Netflix, Warner Bros., Universal, Disney, Formula 1) and produces ticketed physical experiences. Core team of ~20, outsources creative production to partners like LETSGO (Spain). Operates primarily through Immerse LDN at ExCeL London.

**Their strengths:**
- Access to top-tier IP: Squid Game, Friends, Batman Unmasked, Jurassic World, Formula 1
- Sony Music Masterworks backing provides capital and global distribution network (30+ countries)
- The Friends Experience: 950,000+ attendees, 4.6/5 Google rating
- Owned distribution via The Luna Cinema (45+ UK venues, 275,000 annual capacity)
- Founder George Wood named blooloop top 50 most influential in attractions

**Their weaknesses:**
- Loss-making: pre-tax loss of -L1.62M on L7.64M revenue (FY2024); gross margin collapsed from 28.4% to 12.3%
- Revenue declined 44.5% YoY (L13.78M to L7.64M)
- Owns zero IP -- 100% licensed, capturing only operational margin
- Squid Game: The Experience widely panned: "scoring system was completely arbitrary," games "very simple," "overpriced for 45 minutes," "less content than even the most disappointing escape room"
- Equity union filed formal complaint alleging workers denied basic employment rights (Jan 2026)
- Pop-up model with no residual value -- sets dismantled after 6-12 months
- Performs well with passive IP (Friends photo ops) but fails when IP requires actual gameplay

**When prospects mention them:** "Netflix did a Squid Game experience. Can you do something like that with our IP?"

**Vixio's counter-positioning:** Luna proves that blockbuster IP and corporate money do not fix bad game design. Their Squid Game experience had Netflix's brand, Sony's funding, and a professional production partner -- and audiences still called it "garbage" because the games were not fun to play. We design the games first. IP is the skin, not the skeleton. If the mechanics do not create tension, agency, and consequence, no amount of brand recognition saves the experience.

**Key differentiator:** Luna rents IP without design craft. We build the game system that makes IP worth experiencing.

**Data points:** L7.64M revenue (-44.5% YoY), L1.62M pre-tax loss, 12.3% gross margin, ~20 FT employees, 0 owned IP, Squid Game: ~2-3 star reviews, Friends: 950K+ attendees / 4.6 rating, Sony acquired majority Sept 2021.

## 5. vs INCUBASE / Ambient Art Exhibitions (Hong Kong)

**What they do:** INCUBASE operates a permanent anime cultural hub in Mong Kok (20,000+ sq ft) with rotating licensed exhibitions (Chainsaw Man, One Piece, Attack on Titan, Dragon Ball). The broader category includes Van Gogh Alive (touring projection show, 9M+ visitors globally) and similar ambient digital art exhibitions like the now-closed Arte M and Anima Tokyo.

**Their strengths:**
- INCUBASE: established HK presence since 2021, strategic K11 partnership (April 2025), expanding globally (KL, Taipei, Jeju), 30-40% YoY revenue growth
- Van Gogh Alive: proven touring model across 100+ cities, 9M+ global visitors, HK$128-190/pp
- Strong Instagram/social media appeal -- visually striking environments for photos
- Low operational complexity -- observation-based, no game mechanics to manage
- INCUBASE rotating exhibitions keep content fresh (2-3 month cycles)

**Their weaknesses:**
- Zero interactivity -- observation-based, walk-through-and-photograph format
- Van Gogh Alive Klook reviews: 4.1/5, "passive experience," "no real paintings," some feel misled by marketing
- Arte M (similar format) closed Dec 2024 after 2 years -- TripAdvisor: "Cool but just projection on wall -- not worth the price"
- Anima Tokyo (HK$200M investment) closed after 6.5 months -- could not cover HK$7M/month Canton Road rent
- Audiences increasingly reject passive projection spectacle -- "the tolerance for half-measures vanished somewhere around 2024"
- No game mechanics, no cooperative play, no measurable engagement beyond dwell time
- INCUBASE exhibitions are merchandise + display, not interactive experiences

**When prospects mention them:** "Van Gogh Alive did well here. We want an immersive exhibition for our venue."

**Vixio's counter-positioning:** Ambient exhibitions are one-visit Instagram moments. People walk through, take photos, and never return. Five major immersive projection venues closed in the last two years because audiences are done paying to watch walls. We design experiences people play -- cooperative games with cinematic production where the audience has agency, stakes, and a reason to come back. Your venue gets repeat visits and measurable foot traffic data, not a 6-month photo backdrop.

**Key differentiator:** Ambient exhibitions create spectators. We create players who return.

**Data points:** Van Gogh Alive: 100+ cities, 9M visitors, HK$128-190/pp, Klook 4.1/5. Anima Tokyo: HK$200M invested, closed in 6.5 months. Arte M: closed Dec 2024. INCUBASE: 20,000+ sq ft Mong Kok, HK$88-168/exhibition, K11 partnership signed April 2025, expanding to 4 international hubs.
